Established in 1934

The Great Smoky Mountains National Park is a 522,427 acre large subrange of the Appalachian Mountains located along the border of Tennessee and North Carolina, and is the most visited national park in the United States. Named after the natural fog that rolls throughout the range, the Smokies have many beautiful sights to see. With several miles of trails, streams, and roads, there's plenty to explore in the Great Smoky Mountains National Park.

Camping

There are plenty of places to camp across 1000 campsites in 10 campgrounds, and more than 100 backcountry campsites.

Fishing

With 67 unique species of fish throughout 730 miles of fish-bearing streams and 1300 miles of tributaries, there are plenty fish to catch.

Hiking

From mountain views, to waterfalls, forests, and flora, there’s something to see along many hikes in the Smoky Mountains.

Wildlife

With the largest black bear population on the eastern side of the Mississippi and a variety of species, you're sure to see something amazing.

History

With many historical buildings throughout the park, there's a rich history with plenty to learn dating back to before the Civil War.
